RAASTA - Ratings as a Service

RAASTA is a set of high-performing APIs, allowing your team to quickly add User-Ratings support (track and display) to your applications. Out-of-the box, RAASTA includes both high-level javascript client and low-level REST API access, so you can choose how to integrate RAASTA into your app.

Learn more »

Easy

RAASTA's client-side Javascript library wraps everything you need to quickly add user-ratings to your web-app. Run these functions directly in your front-end code, without having to change anything in your backend.

Flexible

Do you need more control? Don't want to expose your tokens? Call RAASTA directly from your backend stack, using standarized REST APIs to access the complete functionality of RAASTA.

Your info is safe

You can choose to pass your system's internal ids however you want. Use secret hashed ids, salted+encrypted ids, or your actual database ids: You set the rules.

Quick Start: Integrating the client-side library

The client-side library lets you STORE and RETRIEVE user-ratings directly from your client-side app. For example, allow your users to rate items with 1-5 stars, and show previous user's ratings in your listing. You can choose to use a 1-5 stars rating system, or any other numeric range.

Fig. 1: Two users rate different items and visualize result in listing.

Get your Access Token

Register your organization and project to receive your access-token. Both the client-side library and the API call will require this token.

Register »

Gather user-ratings

Call RAASTA's rate function from your custom input component, or include one of the predefined user-rating UI components. You can store ratings for a specific item, but also for its category, its parent structure or any other relation.

View tutorial »

Display user-ratings

Once your users have rated items, you can display past ratings in your items listing or detail views. The get call will get the user-rating average for a specific item. You can choose to show its numeric value or alter the style of a rating's graphic representation.

Usage: Client-side Javascript Library

  • Store user-ratings

    High-level Javascript wrapper to call low-level POST requests.

    • Calling raasta.rate(...)
    • will submit a new user-rating for item [ITEM_ID], and
    • call the success [SUCCESS] callback.

    Try it: Submit a new rating for item "demo-item-id-123"

    ...

    Source:
    <script src="https://raasta.herokuapp.com/javascript/raasta.js"></script>
    <script>
      /* Init must be called before any other RAASTA method. You only need it ONCE per page-load. */
      raasta.init( [ACCESS_TOKEN] , [USER_ID] );
    </script>

    <script>
      /* Store user-rating for user [USER_ID] about item [ITEM_ID] : */
      raasta.rate(
         rating: [NUMERIC_RATING] ,
         key1:[ITEM_ID] , // optional
        success:[SUCCESS_CALLBACK] // optional
      });
    </script>

    A User-Rating for item or service [ITEM_ID] will be stored.

  • Retrieve and display user-ratings' average

    High-level Javascript wrapper to call low-level GET requests.

    • Calling raasta.get(...)
    • will load the user-rating average of item [ITEM_ID],
    • set the content of DOM element [DISPLAY_ELEMENT_ID], and
    • set the style of DOM element [STYLE_ELEMENT_ID].

    <script src="https://raasta.herokuapp.com/javascript/raasta.js"></script>

    </script>

Usage: Low-level REST API requests

  • Store user-ratings

    Call this low-level end-point from website or backend.

    curl -X PUT "https://raasta.heroku.com/api/v1/ratings?token=[ACCESS_TOKEN]&user=[USER_ID]&rating=[NUMERIC_RATING]&key1=[ITEM_ID]"

    A User-Rating for item or service [ITEM_ID] will be stored.

  • Retrieve average user-ratings for an item or service (Low-level GET request)

    Retrieve the average user-rating for a specific item, so you can pass the value to your frontend/UI.

    curl -X GET "https://raasta.heroku.com/api/v1/ratings? token=[ACCESS_TOKEN] & key1=[ITEM_ID] "

    Retrieved statistics of User-Ratings for item or service [ITEM_ID] will be returned.

About RAASTA

Motivation

  • Along our careers, we've witnessed multiple teams struggling to solve the user-ratings problem in an effective, efficient way. RAASTA intends to summarize these learnings, by focusing on performance and simplicity.
  • RAASTA was conceived as an experimental project, to measure the Time-To-Market of a new service based on NodeJS+Express, and to test the performance of NodeJS with a Postgres DB.

Tech Stack and Development Practices

  • Server: NodeJS (v5.7) and Express (v4.x)
    • Mocha (2.x) and Chai (2.x) for TDD
  • Database: Postgres (v9.x) with an Append-Only data model
  • Hosting: Heroku
  • Monitoring: NewRelic APM
  • Logging: Papertrail
  • CI: Wercker

License and Legal Agreement

  • By using your access token, you agree to consume this service at your own risk.
  • Be aware that RAASTA is in a pretty early stage: Don't connect it to life monitors or bet your marriage on it (yet).
  • You own your data: we will soon add endpoints to export it.
  • Given you are only providing the IDs of your internal entities and user representation, RAASTA does not keep any copy of your data beyond these ratings.
